Faculty questions
What faculty want to know upfront.
Will this work with my Canvas LMS?
+
Yes. Every artifact embeds in Canvas via iframe and is tested against Canvas's default Content Security Policy. If your institution's IT has locked down external iframes, you receive the source files to host on your own infrastructure. The accessible companion document drops directly into Canvas Pages.
Do I have to redesign my whole course, or can I add TBL to a class I've already built?
+
No, you don't have to redesign anything. Most faculty start by adding one TBL module to a course they've already built, often replacing a single lecture or unit. Project Lab is designed for incremental adoption: build one module, test it with students, then decide whether to expand. Everything else in your course stays exactly as it is.
What exactly do I receive after a Project Lab session?
+
Three things: a deployable interactive HTML artifact with a live URL ready for Canvas, an accessible companion document, and the underlying source files. You walk out of the 90-minute session with a working module, not a draft.
Is the output accessible? WCAG 2.1 and Section 508?
+
We design every artifact to meet WCAG 2.1 and Section 508 standards. The interactive artifact uses semantic HTML, color contrast, keyboard navigation, and alt-text. Every deliverable includes a companion document with full text content and screen-reader-friendly structure. We do not certify formal compliance, since institutional accessibility standards and audit processes vary. If your institution's accessibility review identifies issues, we will revise the artifact at no additional cost or provide an alternative deliverable that meets your institutional requirements.
Do I need any technical skills?
+
None. You don't touch code, GitHub, or any AI prompt directly. You bring your content and learning objectives. We build the artifact during the live session, you review and refine it, then we publish to a live URL ready for Canvas.
Who owns what I create?
+
You do. Every artifact you build belongs to you. Your content, your learning objectives, your final say on edits. You can use it across courses, share it with your department, or include it in your accreditation portfolio.
How is this different from using ChatGPT myself?
+
Two things. First, TBL methodology is built into how each artifact is structured: readiness assurance, application exercises, and peer accountability are designed into the output rather than bolted on. Second, you're not prompting alone. You're working alongside someone who knows both the pedagogy and the tooling, so the result is a defensible TBL artifact, not a generic worksheet.
